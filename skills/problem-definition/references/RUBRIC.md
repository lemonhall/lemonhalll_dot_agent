# Rubric (Problem Definition Pack)

Score each category 0/1/2:
- 0 = missing / unclear
- 1 = present but incomplete
- 2 = clear, testable, decision-ready

Suggested passing bar: **>= 12/16**.

## 1) Clarity of problem (0–2)
- **0:** No problem statement, or the statement embeds a specific solution/technology (e.g., “We need an AI chatbot”).
- **1:** 1-liner exists and is solution-free, but the expanded statement lacks impact quantification or lists causes as facts rather than hypotheses.
- **2:** 1-liner is specific and solution-free; expanded statement explains impact with evidence, lists causes as labeled hypotheses, and includes “why now.”

## 2) Target segment + context (0–2)
- **0:** No segment defined, or segment is “all users” / “everyone.”
- **1:** Primary segment is named but not recruitable (e.g., “power users” without behavioral criteria); situation/context is vague.
- **2:** Primary segment is explicit and recruitable (behavior + recency criteria); situation/context describes when/where/why the problem happens and what's at stake.

## 3) JTBD quality (0–2)
- **0:** No JTBD section, or jobs are just feature requests in disguise.
- **1:** Primary job exists but sub-jobs are missing or exceed 5 without scoping; user success criteria are vague.
- **2:** Primary job + 2-5 sub-jobs are coherent and outcome-oriented; user success criteria are defined and distinct from product metrics.

## 4) Alternatives + differentiation (0–2)
- **0:** No alternatives listed, or only direct competitors mentioned (no workarounds/analog).
- **1:** Alternatives include some workarounds but switching costs or “why switch” rationale is missing; no analog/non-digital option considered.
- **2:** Alternatives include real workarounds (including analog/non-digital when relevant); switching costs and “why switch” rationale are explicit for each.

## 5) Evidence + assumptions (0–2)
- **0:** No evidence/assumptions log, or everything is listed as “known” without sources.
- **1:** Known vs unknown is separated, but top assumptions lack proposed tests or all are marked “high confidence” without evidence.
- **2:** Known vs unknown is clear with cited sources; top assumptions each have a proposed test, confidence level, and at least one concrete evidence artifact.

## 6) Metrics + guardrails (0–2)
- **0:** No metrics, or only vanity metrics (e.g., “user happiness”) with no measurement method.
- **1:** Outcome metric exists but is unmeasurable within the stated timeline, or guardrails are missing.
- **2:** Outcome metric(s) and leading indicators are defined, measurable within timeline, and include baselines; guardrails prevent gaming or harm.

## 7) Scope boundaries (0–2)
- **0:** No in/out list; scope is unbounded.
- **1:** In/out exists but non-goals are vague or dependencies are not captured.
- **2:** In/out and non-goals are explicit and specific; dependencies and constraints are captured with owners or resolution plans.

## 8) Prototype/learning plan (0–2)
- **0:** No prototype or learning plan; jumps straight to “build it.”
- **1:** End state is described but the test plan is generic (“we'll test it”) or doesn't target the hardest assumptions.
- **2:** End state is visible (“see the end from the beginning”); test plan is the fastest path to learning, targets the 1-3 biggest unknowns, and has clear success criteria.
